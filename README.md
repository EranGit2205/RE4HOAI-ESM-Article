# RE4HOAI Supplementary Materials

This repository contains the electronic supplementary materials for the article:

**How Teams Decide on Human Oversight in AI-Powered Systems: A Mixed-Methods Study**

The article presents **RE4HOAI**, a lifecycle-oriented Requirements Engineering framework for defining, negotiating, and revisiting human-oversight requirements in AI-powered systems. The supplementary materials provide additional evidence, methodological details, supporting analyses, and data artifacts related to the framework construction, expert refinement, and survey-based assessment.

## Study overview

The study followed a design-science and exploratory sequential mixed-methods logic. The supplementary materials correspond to the main stages of the research:

1. **Conceptual grounding**
   The study builds on the OG-RE4AI framework, which organizes AI-system requirements across requirement categories, lifecycle stages, and stakeholder roles.

2. **Problem-space grounding and initial framework construction**
   Literature-based cases and stakeholder-interview analysis were used to identify recurring human-oversight tensions, including automation versus control, performance versus explainability, transparency versus complexity, and trust versus automation.

3. **Expert refinement**
   A panel of experts reviewed the initial RE4HOAI framework. Their feedback refined the framework’s phases, concepts, decision points, and operational applicability.

4. **Survey-based assessment**
   A survey of stakeholders was used to assess the perceived clarity, relevance, and usefulness of the refined RE4HOAI framework.

## How to use these materials

Readers may use these files to:

* inspect the conceptual and empirical grounding of RE4HOAI;
* review the literature-based case analysis;
* examine the questionnaire and interview-protocol material;
* trace how stakeholder-interview themes informed framework construction;
* review the expert-feedback evidence behind the final framework refinements;
* inspect supporting MCDA/trade-off analysis materials;
* examine the survey-based assessment data and analysis.

## Notes

These materials are provided as supplementary evidence for the article and should be interpreted together with the manuscript. The survey results support perceived clarity, relevance, and usefulness of the framework, but they do not constitute field validation or evidence of implementation feasibility in all organizational contexts.
